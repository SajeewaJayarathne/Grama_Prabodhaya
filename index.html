GRAMA PRABODHAYA 2016/17

Grama Prabodhaya is a community service initiative by the Rotaract Club of University of Moratuwa. The primary aim is to create a platform via Rotaract Mora to help rural villages in Sri Lanka with burning problems who need our care and support immensely. This is not just a community service project with the concept of material donation but a collective effort to develop a village comprehensively in many aspects to uplift the quality of life of the villagers. Grama Prabodhaya 2016/17 will be executed under several phases which include tailor made mini projects addressing the key prevailing issues in the village Ranugalla in the Monaragala district. The ultimate intention of executing this series of projects focussed around a rural village is to develop the village in multiple aspects and help them to sustain a better quality of life.

Our Mission

Along with Grama Prabodhaya project, we hope to cater the following aspects from the complementary mini projects attached to it.
• Enhance basic education and literacy.
• Improve the infrastructure within the school premises.
• Improve the facilities needed for sports and the library.
• Enhance the mentality and motivation of students and villagers.
• Disease prevention and improving treatment facilities in the village.
• Providing access for clean water and enhancing sanitation in the village.

Our Vision

Grama Prabodhaya team hopes to build and strengthen the connections with the chosen village, Ranugalla through the temple and the school where the crowd attraction is comparatively high.
This project is being introduced not only for developing the village Ranugalla, but also to help the rotaractors to enhance the bond among each other and illuminate the image of Rotaract Mora.
We believe that this can be a great exemplar for other community service clubs to serve the community through social service ventures of this nature.

Ranugalla Village

Ranugalla is a rural village situated in Monaragala district. It is a place of great historic importance due to ancient legends revolving around King Ravana. It is said to be one of the places used by the King Ravana to place his spies for military intelligence activities. Moreover, historian Dr. Mirando Obeysekara had been visiting this village for several research projects due to its historical importance.

At present, this village gives the shelter for nearly 200 families. A temple has been built on the land of King Ravana and it is named after him; Ravanaaraamaya. However, only one thero lives in there who has been trying his best to help and direct the villagers towards a bright path, for his entire life.

According to the survey of the Grama Prabodhaya Committee, it is evident that villages in Ranugalla gives a very prominent place for education. Though the Ranugalla Kanishta Vidyalaya is the only school they have for the village, they are taking the maximum advantage out of it. Hence, to give a good education for the children in the village, all the families are willing to help and develop the only school they have in every aspect they are capable of.

About Rotaract Mora

Rotaract club of University of Moratuwa also named as Rotaract Mora is the first university-based Rotaract Club of Sri Lanka which was initiated in 1995 and has successfully marked 21 years of service. The club was originated with a base of 16 members and has now expanded extensively with 200+ membership, turning into a club with the biggest enrollment in the university representing all three faculties. Rotaract Mora is sponsored by the Rotary Club of Colombo West and is chartered under Rotaract District 3220.

Without deviating focus on the main objective of community services, the club has widened its scope in the avenues of professional development, international understanding and club services. Rotaract Mora completes over 50 projects every year empowering members to display their professional and dynamic skills while emerging knowledge and increasing awareness of the necessities and the issues in the society. Living by the Rotaract motto ‘Service above self’ is the club's primary objective in providing and improving the standards of the community around us. Rotaract Mora provides a platform for the members to instigate new projects according to the needs of the society.

Sub-Projects under Phase 1

Science Laboratory and Library

Building of a fully equipped science laboratory and a library for the school. The laying of the foundation stone was held on the 11th of December, 2016. Along with the science laboratory and the library another similar room is being constructed to be used as a classroom. This building will be opened on the 29th April, 2017.

Intellect

A series of seminars conducted for Mathematics, Science, English Language, and Sinhala Language and Literature targeting the students who sat for the GCE O/L examination in December 2016. Further, a counseling and a motivational programme were also been integrated with the seminar series which were conducted by the Rotaractors.

Healing Hands

A full-day medical camp held on 11th December, 2016 by treating patients in the Ranugalla and Passara areas. The medical camp comprised of general checkups and consultation, dental, random and fasting blood sugar check-up faciilities, and a pharmacy. Two sessions on non-communicable diseases and nutrition were also integrated into the event to educate the villagers on these areas.

Bridge of Hearts

A sub project that donates books for the newly constructed library of the Ranugalla Kanishta Vidyalaya. More than 1000 books and an enormous amount of monetary funds were raised from the collection points arranged within the University premises and at the Bandaranaike Memorial International Conference Hall during the International Book Fair 2016.

Lead with Spark

A leadership development camp organized in collaboration with the Prefects' Guild of Ananda College, Colombo, while having the intention of unveiling the leader inside the students of Ranugalla Kanishta Vidyalaya, was held on 8th April, 2017. This included a few motivational sessions, an informative session, and experience sharing and team bonding activities.

Beyond Boundaries

A volleyball coaching camp organized for the students in grades 10 and 11 of the Ranugalla Kanishta Vidyalaya, which was held on the 9th April, 2017. This event included a motivational session and informative sessions on health and food, injuries, and medical knowledge. The camp was concluded with a friendly volleyball match played between the Rotaractors and the school volleyball team.

Rainbow of Hope

The painting session of the newly constructed building which was held in two phases. The first phase was held on 8th and 9th April, 2017 where the Rotaractors got together to paint the walls. The second phase was concluded with the completion of the painting session by the hands of the employees of London Stock Exchange Group, who is the main sponsor of the project.

Inspirer

An English language usage and communication skills enhancement program focused on grade 9 to 11 students in Ranugalla area, which was held on 21st and 22nd of April, 2017. This program consists of two phases where the first phase is a two-day workshop and next is the establishment of student communities in each school.

Akurata Aruthak

A literary competition organized for the students in Ranugalla Kanishta Vidyalaya. The competition is on Poster, Drawing, Essay Writing, and Poem Writing under four different age categories.
